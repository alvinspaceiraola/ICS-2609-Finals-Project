Visa

Tourist Visa

  • 60-day Tourist Visa to Thailand
  • Can either be a multiple or single entry
  • Can be extended for up 30 days by means of a visa run


Non-Immigrant Visa

  • Normally a single entry visa valid for 90 days
  • Stay can be extended through visa run.
  • Obtaining a work permit and opening a bank account is possible.


Business Visa

  • For people who wants to do business legally in Thailand.
  • Also known as Non-Immigrant Business Visa.
  • Needs a visa run every 90 days.
  • Work permits are obtainable, opening a bank account is possible.


Thai Elite Visa

  • Categorized under Privilege Entry Visa
  • Period between 5 and 20 years

Best Time to Go

The best time to travel to Thailand is between late November and early April when there is little rain and lots of sun, but the temperature is cooler.


Weather

Thailand experiences a rainy season from July to October, the rest are good and sunny


Getting Around

The best way to get around Bangkok is by Skytrain or Metro, which is fast, affordable, and easily navigable for visitors. The city also offers an extensive network of buses, but these are prone to traffic jams, which the city is notorious for. Taxis are fairly cheap, but again, gridlock is a regular issue. However, taxis are the easiest way to get from Bangkok's Suvarnabhumi Airport (BKK) to downtown.

  • Bus

    • Bangkok Mass Transit Authority runs an extensive network of buses throughout the city. tickets range from 6.50 baht (about $0.20) to 23 baht (about $0.60).

  • Skytrain and Metro

    • Rates for the Skytrain run between 15 and 42 baht (about $0.50 and $1.30) depending on your destination, while one-day passes (for unlimited rides) cost 140 baht (roughly $4.20). The Metro (MRT) canvasses a smaller section of the city. The MRT has its own rates that are similar in price to Skytrain.


Popular Attractions in Bangkok


Grand Palace

If you only visit one major historical tourist attraction in Bangkok, this should be the one. The royal compound lives up to its name, with spectacular structures that would put the most decadent modern monarchs to shame. It's also the home of Wat Phra Kaeo, which houses the Jade (or Emerald) Buddha.


SEA LIFE Bangkok Ocean World

In SEA LIFE Bangkok Ocean World, you can get up close to over 400 water species of Bangkok's most famous attractions, be able to meet and greet adorable penguins, otters, water rats, and many more. You can also experience a unique fish pedicure and explore a glass-bottom boat ride through the main tank.
