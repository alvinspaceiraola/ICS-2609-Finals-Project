Luang Prabang

Currency

Lao Kip (LAK) '₭' or '₭N'


Language

Laotian


Visa

Tourist Visa (30 days)

  • You can extend the visa up to 60 days through the department of Immigration in Vientiane.

Business Visa (1 month)

  • Visa authorization is arranged by sponsor in Laos and the Embassy cannot process visas until it receives authorization or approval from the authorities concerned in Laos.

Transit Visa (5 Days)

  • Maximum 5 days for stay (in Vientiane only). Proof of an entry visa and air ticket to the third country.

Visit Visa (1 month)

  • Can be extended for two additional months. Visa request can be processed either through a guarantor in Laos or the Laos Embassy


Electric

All power sockets in Laos provide a standard voltage of 230V with a standard frequency of 50Hz. You can use all your equipment in Laos if the outlet voltage in your own country is between 220V-240V.


Timezone

Indochina Time (GMT +7)

Best Time to Go

The most popular time to visit is October to April.


Weather

The rainy season can be pretty cold and depending on the day and the altitude, there can be torrential rainfall. During this time, the Laotians anticipate the verdant fields and jungles to be replenished around then. However, for visitors, the monsoon season only has strong rain for small intervals of time. It can rain hard pellets for an hour and then the clouds will clear and the sun will shine. In Luang Prabang, August is the wettest month.
November to May - Cooler temperature.
June to September - Heavy rainfall with the southwest monsoon.
August - Wettest Month.
March to May - hottest time of the year.


Getting Around

The tuk tuks are available everywhere. So no troubles there. They tend to want to charge more than Vientiane, but of course negotiable. Bikes are available everywhere, usually including the hotels, and walking is quite cheap. Hats a necessity and umbrellas (fellas do not be proud) are wondrerful . Transport out to the waterfull by van or tuk tuk on good road and about 45 minutes. The falls are good and the water is clean. Would reccommend an pair of sandles or old sneakers to go in the water as in many parts quite rocky and slippery. The bus and or van to or fro Vientiane is a nine hour trip at best. Over the mountains very windy (very) for half and very bumpy the other half. Not great sights and lot of stops. The flight, though more expensive, cost around 90-100 but takes just on 40 minutes. Could be the best money you spend.


Popular Attractions in Luang Prabang


Wat Xieng Thong

Wat Xieng Thong is one of the largest temples in Luang Prabang. A symbol of great historic importance, its structure has characteristics of 16th-century Lao architecture, with elaborate mosaic patterns, wall carvings, rare Buddhist deities, and a 12-metre-tall funeral carriage.


Kuang Si Butterfly Park

Kuang Si Butterfly Park is a project that was initiated in January 2014 from 2 Dutch passionate people from Holland. The mission is to create a research centre studying and publishing about Laos butterflies, host plants and preservation because of environmental issues in Laos.
