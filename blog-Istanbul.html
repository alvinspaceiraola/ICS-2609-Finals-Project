Currency

Language

Visa

Single Entry Visa

  • Allows the holder to enter Turkey once, and stay in the country for up to three months (90 days). The visa is valid for use for one year from the date of issue.

Multiple Entry Visa

  • Valid for up to five years and allows the holder to make multiple visits of up to three months each time.

Transit Visa

  • Valid for up to three months and permits travel through Turkish territory.


Electric

Turkey operates on 220 volts, 50 Hz, with round-prong European-style plugs that fit into recessed wall sockets /points.


Timezone

Turkey Time (GMT+3)

Best Time to Go

The best times to visit Istanbul are from March to May and between September and November. That's when crowds at the city's attractions are manageable, room rates are average and daytime temperatures generally sit in the 60s and 70s. Peak season – from June to August – sees temps soar into the low 60s to low 80s, and accommodation prices increase to match the demand from incoming tourists. December through February, meanwhile, are the cheapest months to visit, but Istanbul's rainy, snowy and chilly conditions (temps are in the high 30s to high 50s) mean you'll have to don cold weather attire and lug an umbrella during your stay.


Weather

The most comfortable months to visit are from September to December, their Autumn time. During Summer temperatures can go up to 30°C which is from June until September. Spring time starts from March until May, the air will be colder but not as humid as the summer season and lasts from March until June. Lastly, Winter in Istanbul is from December to March with the temperature averaging 4°C. Snowfalls tend to be heavy, but the snow cover and temperatures below the freezing point rarely last more than a few days.s


Getting Around

In order to use all public transportation in Istanbul (boats, subways, buses, tramways, funiculars), you will need the magnetic card (Istanbul Kart). You can find it in the small kiosks near all metro stations, piers, and also bus stations. It costs 7 TL, you will then add credit. Keep in mind that most trips cost 3,50 TL each way. If you buy a card with a limited number of trips, you can do it directly in front of the turnstiles. The prices are 7 TL for one way, 11 TL for two ways, 15 TL for three ways, 23 TL for five ways, and 40 TL for ten ways.


Popular Attractions in Istanbul


The Blue Mosque

The Blue Mosque is famous for the intricate blue tile work that covers the interior of the building. It is free to enter, and if it is your first time inside a mosque The Blue Mosque is a good one to start. There are a few rules that need to be followed when entering the mosque, since there are a large number of tourists that visit the mosque on a daily basis, the entrance procedures are well explained here.


Hagia Sophia

Hagia Sophia (Ayasofya in Turkish) can be considered as the best and the most visited sights in Istanbul together with the neighboring Topkapi Palace. Hagia Sophia is a former church and museum and declared as one of the world’s greatest architectural works and accepted as the 8th wonder of the world.
